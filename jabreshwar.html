Jabreshwar Temple

The Jabreshwar Mandir in Phaltan, Maharashtra, is a quaint Shiva temple with a rich history. It was built by the Marathas over an existing Jain temple. The temple is made of stone and features beautiful carvings. Visitors going to the palace or the Ram Mandir often find it hard to miss.

History: The Jabreshwar Mandir is an ancient Shiva temple that has stood the test of time. It was originally built by the Marathas on the site of an existing Jain temple. The temple’s architecture showcases intricate stone carvings, reflecting the craftsmanship of its builders.

Origin and Architecture: The temple was built by the Marathas over an existing Jain temple. The temple is constructed entirely from stone, featuring beautiful carvings. Its unique aspect is that it was carved out of a single rock around 1237 A.D. The Jabreshwar Mandir stands as a testament to ancient craftsmanship and devotion

Location: The temple is situated in Phaltan, Maharashtra, India. Visitors often come across it while exploring nearby attractions such as the Ram Mandir and the Phaltan Palace.

Devotion: Jabreshwar Mandir continues to be an active place of worship. Devotees visit to seek blessings from Lord Shiva and immerse themselves in spiritual contemplation.

Visiting Experience: On average, visitors spend about 10-15 minutes at the temple. The serene ambiance and historical significance make it a worthwhile stop during your exploration of Phaltan
