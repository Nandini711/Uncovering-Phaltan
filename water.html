Dhumalwadi Waterfall

Dhumalwadi Waterfall is a beautiful spot near Phaltan in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India.

Location: Dhumalwadi is a village located in the Phaltan taluka of Satara district. It lies approximately 27 kilometers from Phaltan town.

Scenic Beauty: As you approach Dhumalwadi, the scenery transforms from barren villages to lush greenery. The area is particularly enchanting during the monsoon season. The route to the waterfall is scenic, offering wonderful views along the way.

How to Reach: Take the Phaltan Girvi Road towards Girvi. Pass through villages like Nirgudi and Dhudhebavi. Just before Girvi, take a left turn towards Dhumalwadi. Enter Dhumalwadi through a small ghat. Park your vehicles in the village and continue walking inside the hills. The road leads through a small river with flowing water. You won’t need to climb steep hills; the walk is relatively short.

The Waterfall: The main waterfall is visible without any strenuous climbing. During the monsoon, the falls are at their best. There are actually more than two falls, all visible from the viewpoint. For a frontal view of the main fall, continue in the same direction for about 100 meters until you reach the hill/mountain where the road seems to end. If you proceed further, you’ll reach Kund.

Additional Information: Dhumalwadi is not only about the waterfall; it’s also a hill station. The area offers a refreshing escape from city life.
