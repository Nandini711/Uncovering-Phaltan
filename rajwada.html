Mudhoji Manmohan Rajwada

Take a trip to Mudhoji Manmohan Rajwada, an old palace in the city of Phaltan in Maharashtra, India. The palace was once the maternal home of Sambhaji Maharaj, son of the founder of the Maratha Empire, who succeeded the realm after his father’s death. The compound consists of residential buildings, several temples, spacious courtyards, and long halls.

The interior reflects the architectural style of the period, from the intricate hand-carved woodwork seen in the columns and arches to the rich color palette in the paintings and tapestries. All of the rooms in the palace open out onto a central courtyard, or chowk, demonstrating a key feature in Indian architecture, allowing cool air to flow throughout the various rooms. The opulence of this royal home along with its architectural wonder makes it a very sought after attraction for tourists visiting the town of Phaltan. Officially named Mudhoji Manmohan Rajwada, the Phaltan Palace is a 19th century home located in the city of Phaltan, Maharashtra, India.


Name and Ruler: The palace is formally known as the Madhoji Manmohan Rajwada, named after the ruler who built it. Mudhoji Manmohan ruled the Phaltan State from 10th February 1860 to 17th October 1916.

Historical Significance: magnificent palace was once the maternal home of Sambhaji Maharaj, the son of the founder of the Maratha Empire. Sambhaji Maharaj succeeded the realm after his father’s death.

Architectural Blend: The Phaltan Rajwada is an amalgamation of Western architecture with Jain and Hindu styles. It represents the transition brought about by the rulers of Phaltan as they entered the modern era. The architectural style is Indo-Saracenic, with a touch of Gothic influence.

Construction Timeline: The construction of the palace occurred in two parts. The first part began in 1861 and was completed in 1875. The second phase continued until 1911.
